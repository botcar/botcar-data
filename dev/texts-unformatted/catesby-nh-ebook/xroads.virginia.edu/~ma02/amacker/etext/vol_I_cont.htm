  1. Aquila Capite Albo: The Bald Eagle.

  2. Accipter Piscatorious: The Fishing Hawk.

  3. Accipter Palumbarius: The Pigeon Hawk.

  4. Accipter Cauda furcata: The Swallow-Tail Hawk.

  5. Accipter Minor: The Little Hawk.

  6. Buteo, Specie Gallo-Pavonis: The Turkey Buzzard.

  7. Noctua Aurita Minor: The Little Owl.

  8. Caprimulgus: The Goat-Sucker of Carolina.

  9. Cuculus Caroliniensis: The Cuckow of Carolina.
    Castanea pumila Virginiana, fructu racemoto parvo in singulis capsulis echinatis unico: The Chinkapin.

  10. Psitticus Paradisi ex Cuba: The Parrot of Paradise of Cuba.

  11. Psitticus Caroliniensis: The Parrot of Carolina.

  12. Mondela Purpurea: The Purple Jack-Daw.

  13. Sturnus niger alis superne rubentibus: The red-wing'd starling.
    Myrtus Brabanticae similis Caroliniensis humilior; foliis latioribus & magis ferratis: The broad-leaved Candle-berry MYRTLE.

  14. Hortulanus Caroliniensis: The Rice-Bird.

  15. Pica glandaria caerulea cristata: The Blue Jay.
    Smilax laevis Lauri folio baccis nigris: The Bay-Leaved Smilax.

  16. Picus Maximus rostro albo: The largest white-bill Wood-pecker.
    Quercus, an potius Ilex Marilandica folio longo angusto Salicus. Raii Hist: The Willow-Oak.

  17. PICUS niger maximus capite rubro: The larger red-crested Wood-pecker.
    Quercus sempervivens foliis oblongis non sinuatis. D. Banister: The Live Oak.

  18. PICUS major alis aureis: The Gold-winged Wood-pecker.
    Quercus castaneae foliis, procera arbor virginiana: The Chestnut Oak.

  19. Pica Ventre rubro: The Red-bellied Wood-pecker.
    Picus medius quasi villosus: The Hairy Wood-pecker.

  20. Picus Capite toto rubro: The Red-headed Wood-pecker.
    Quercus folio non serrato, in summitate quasi triangulo: The Water-Oak.

  21. Picus Various Minor, Ventre Luteo: The yellow belly'd Wood-pecker.
    Picus Varius Minimus: The Smallest Spotted Wood-pecker.
    Quercus alba Virginiana, Park; The White Oak.
    Quercus Carolinensis virentibus venis muricata: The White Oak, with pointed Notches.
    Syringa Baccifera, Myrti subrotundis foliis, floribus albis gemellis ex provincia Floridana.

  22. Sitta Capite Nigro: The Nuthatch.
    Sitta Capite Fusco: The Small Nuthatch.
    Quercus humilior salicis folio breviore: The Highland Willow Oak.

  23. Palumbus Migratorius: The Pigeon of Passage.
    Quercus Esculi divisura foliis amplioribus aculeatis: The Red Oak.

  24. Turtur Carolinensis: The Turtle of Carolina.
    Anapodophyllon Canadense Merini: The May Apple.

  25. Columba Capite Albo: The White-crown'd Pigeon.
    Frutex Cotini fere folio crasso, in summitate deliquium patiente, fructu ovali caeruleo ossiculum angulosom continente: The Cocoa Plum.
